# Market Research Skill

This skill conducts thorough market research to inform digital marketing strategies. It combines web search, data analysis, and strategic thinking to deliver actionable insights.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the user requests:
- Competitive analysis and benchmarking
- Target audience research and persona development
- Keyword research and SEO opportunities
- Market trends and industry insights
- Customer pain points and motivations
- Content gap analysis
- Social media landscape analysis

## Research Process

### 1. Define Research Scope

Start by clarifying:
- **Industry/niche**: What market are we researching?
- **Geographic focus**: Local, regional, national, or global?
- **Key competitors**: Who are the main players? (up to 5-7 competitors)
- **Target audience**: Demographics, behaviors, interests
- **Research objectives**: What decisions will this research inform?

### 2. Competitive Intelligence

For each competitor, gather:
- **Digital presence**: Website, social media, content marketing
- **Messaging**: Brand positioning, unique value propositions
- **Tactics**: Ad strategies, content types, engagement methods
- **Strengths/weaknesses**: What they do well, gaps to exploit
- **Market positioning**: How they differentiate themselves

Use web_search to find:
- Competitor websites and about pages
- Recent news and press releases
- Social media profiles and engagement
- Customer reviews and sentiment
- Industry reports mentioning competitors

### 3. Audience Research

Build detailed customer personas:
- **Demographics**: Age, location, income, education, occupation
- **Psychographics**: Values, interests, lifestyle, motivations
- **Online behavior**: Platforms used, content consumed, purchase journey
- **Pain points**: Problems they're trying to solve
- **Language/tone**: How they communicate, terminology they use

Research sources:
- Industry surveys and reports
- Social media discussions and communities
- Reddit, Quora, and forum conversations
- Product reviews and testimonials
- Keyword research for search intent

### 4. Keyword & SEO Analysis

Identify search opportunities:
- **Primary keywords**: High-volume terms (use web_search for current data)
- **Long-tail keywords**: Specific, lower-competition phrases
- **Question-based queries**: "How to...", "What is...", "Best..."
- **Search intent**: Informational, transactional, navigational, commercial
- **Content gaps**: Topics competitors aren't covering well

### 5. Trend Analysis

Identify market dynamics:
- **Emerging trends**: New technologies, consumer behaviors, industry shifts
- **Seasonal patterns**: When is demand highest/lowest?
- **Content trends**: What formats and topics are gaining traction?
- **Platform trends**: Where is the audience migrating?

## Research Best Practices

**Use Multiple Sources**: Cross-reference information from multiple web searches to validate findings.

**Be Specific**: Don't make vague claims. Use actual data, examples, and specific observations.

**Cite Sources**: Include links to sources in the appendix so findings can be verified.

**Focus on Actionable Insights**: Every finding should lead to a strategic implication or recommendation.

**Balance Depth and Breadth**: Cover all key areas without getting lost in excessive detail.

**Update with Fresh Data**: Always search for the most current information, especially for trends and competitor activities.

**Consider Context**: Tailor the depth and focus based on the client's specific needs and constraints.
