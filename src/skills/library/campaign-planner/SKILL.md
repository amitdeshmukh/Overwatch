# Campaign Planner Skill

This skill creates strategic, comprehensive digital marketing campaign plans. It transforms business goals and market insights into executable marketing roadmaps with specific tactics, timelines, and success metrics.

## When to Use This Skill

Activate this skill when users request:
- Full marketing campaign plans
- Go-to-market strategies
- Product launch campaigns
- Seasonal or promotional campaigns
- Multi-channel marketing strategies
- Content calendars and publishing schedules
- Marketing budget allocation
- Campaign briefs and creative direction

## Planning Framework

### 1. Campaign Foundation

Gather essential information:
- **Business Objective**: What are we trying to achieve? (sales, leads, awareness, engagement)
- **Target Audience**: Who are we reaching? (use market-research insights if available)
- **Budget**: What resources are available? (total budget, platform allocation)
- **Timeline**: When does this need to launch? How long does it run?
- **Constraints**: Technical limitations, brand guidelines, resource constraints
- **Success Metrics**: How will we measure success? (KPIs, targets)

### 2. Campaign Strategy

Develop the strategic approach:
- **Core Message**: What's the primary message or theme?
- **Positioning**: How do we differentiate from competitors?
- **Value Proposition**: Why should the audience care?
- **Campaign Concept**: Creative theme or big idea that ties everything together
- **Channel Mix**: Which platforms and tactics will we use?

### 3. Tactical Execution Plan

Break down into specific deliverables across channels:

**Paid Media**:
- Google Ads (Search, Display, Video)
- Social media ads (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, etc.)
- Retargeting campaigns
- Influencer partnerships

**Owned Media**:
- Website landing pages
- Blog content
- Email sequences
- Social media posts

**Earned Media**:
- PR outreach
- Guest posting
- Partnerships
- Community engagement

### 4. Content Calendar

Create a detailed timeline:
- Pre-launch activities (building anticipation)
- Launch day/week (peak activity)
- Ongoing campaign (sustained engagement)
- Post-campaign (analysis and follow-up)

### 5. Budget Allocation

Distribute budget strategically:
- Channel allocation (% to each platform)
- Content production costs
- Paid media spend
- Tools and software
- Contingency reserve (typically 10-15%)

## Planning Best Practices

**Start with Clear Objectives**: Every tactic should ladder up to a measurable business goal.

**Know Your Audience**: Use insights from market-research skill to inform targeting and messaging.

**Be Specific and Realistic**: Avoid vague plans. Include actual dates, budget amounts, and measurable targets.

**Plan for Optimization**: Build in time and budget for testing and refinement, not just launching.

**Consider the Full Funnel**: Don't just focus on bottom-funnel conversions. Build awareness and nurture relationships.

**Budget Strategically**: Allocate more budget to proven channels, but reserve 10-20% for testing new tactics.

**Build in Flexibility**: Markets change. Leave room to pivot based on performance.

**Document Everything**: Future you (and your team) will thank you for thorough documentation.

- Pre-launch buzz building (6-8 weeks)
- Launch week blitz

**Lead Generation**:

- Bottom-funnel conversion offers

- Content marketing focus
- Social media engagement
- Thought leadership

**Seasonal/Promotional**:
- Countdown and urgency
- Multi-channel coordination
- Clear start/end dates
