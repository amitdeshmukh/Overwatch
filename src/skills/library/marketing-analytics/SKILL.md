# Marketing Analytics Skill

This skill transforms raw marketing data into actionable insights. It analyzes campaign performance, identifies optimization opportunities, and creates clear reports that drive strategic decisions.

## When to Use This Skill

Activate this skill for:
- Campaign performance reports
- ROI and ROAS analysis
- Channel attribution and effectiveness
- A/B test result interpretation
- Website analytics insights
- Conversion funnel analysis
- Customer journey mapping
- Marketing dashboard creation
- Monthly/quarterly marketing reviews
- Budget optimization recommendations

## Analytics Framework

### 1. Data Collection & Setup

Before analysis, identify:
- **Data Sources**: Google Analytics, ad platforms, CRM, email platform, etc.
- **Time Period**: What dates are we analyzing?
- **Metrics**: Which KPIs matter for this analysis?
- **Goals**: What questions are we trying to answer?
- **Benchmarks**: What are we comparing against? (previous period, industry, goals)

### 2. Key Metrics by Channel

**Website Analytics**:
- Traffic: Users, sessions, pageviews
- Engagement: Bounce rate, avg. session duration, pages per session
- Conversions: Goal completions, conversion rate, revenue
- Acquisition: Traffic sources, top channels, landing pages

**Paid Advertising**:
- Impressions, clicks, CTR
- Cost metrics: CPC, CPM, CPA
- Conversions and conversion rate
- ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
- Quality Score (Google Ads)

**Email Marketing**:
- Open rate, click-through rate
- Conversion rate, revenue per email
- List growth rate
- Unsubscribe rate
- Engagement over time

**Social Media**:
- Reach and impressions
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Click-through rate
- Follower growth
- Video views and completion rate

**SEO/Organic**:
- Organic traffic and growth
- Keyword rankings
- Backlinks acquired
- Domain authority
- Organic conversions

### 3. Analysis Types

**Performance Analysis**:
- What happened? (descriptive)
- What worked and what didn't?
- How do results compare to goals and benchmarks?

**Diagnostic Analysis**:
- Why did it happen?
- What factors drove performance?
- What obstacles prevented better results?

**Predictive Analysis**:
- What's likely to happen next?
- Trend forecasting
- Projection modeling

**Prescriptive Analysis**:
- What should we do about it?
- Actionable recommendations
- Optimization priorities

## Analytics Best Practices

**Tell a Story with Data**:
- Start with the headline/insight
- Support with specific metrics
- Provide context (comparisons, benchmarks)
- End with actionable recommendations

**Be Specific**:
- Use exact numbers, not ranges
- Include percentages AND absolute values
- Show trends over time
- Compare to benchmarks

**Focus on What Matters**:
- Prioritize metrics tied to business goals
- Don't just list every metric available
- Highlight outliers and anomalies
- Identify correlation vs causation

**Make It Visual**:
- Use tables for comparative data
- Describe charts/graphs that would enhance understanding
- Use formatting to highlight key numbers
- Create visual hierarchy in reports

**Be Honest**:
- Report bad news along with good
- Explain what you don't know
- Acknowledge data limitations
- Don't cherry-pick metrics
