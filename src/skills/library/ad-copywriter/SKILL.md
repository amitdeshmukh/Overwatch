# Ad Copywriter Skill

This skill creates compelling, conversion-focused ad copy for paid advertising campaigns. It applies direct response copywriting principles, platform-specific best practices, and psychological triggers to drive clicks and conversions.

## When to Use This Skill

Activate this skill for:
- Google Search Ads (text ads)
- Google Display Ads
- Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)
- LinkedIn Ads
- Twitter/X Ads
- TikTok Ads
- YouTube Ads
- Pinterest Ads
- Ad variations for A/B testing
- Retargeting ad copy

## Ad Copywriting Framework

### 1. Pre-Writing Research

Gather essential information:
- **Campaign Objective**: Awareness, consideration, or conversion?
- **Target Audience**: Who are we reaching? What are their pain points?
- **Offer**: What are we selling/promoting?
- **Unique Value Prop**: Why should they choose us over competitors?
- **Landing Page**: What happens after they click?
- **Budget/Competition**: How competitive is the auction?
- **Platform**: Which ad platform and format?

### 2. Core Copywriting Principles

**AIDA Framework**:
- **Attention**: Grab them with the headline
- **Interest**: Build curiosity or address pain
- **Desire**: Show the benefit/transformation
- **Action**: Clear CTA that tells them what to do

**Psychological Triggers**:
- Urgency/Scarcity: Limited time, limited spots
- Social Proof: "Join 10,000+ users"
- Authority: Credentials, awards, certifications
- Fear of Missing Out (FOMO): "Don't get left behind"
- Curiosity: "The secret to..." "What nobody tells you..."
- Reciprocity: Free value upfront
- Specificity: Exact numbers beat round numbers

**Benefit-Focused Writing**:
- Features tell, benefits sell
- Focus on outcomes, not just what it is
- Answer "What's in it for me?"

### 3. Platform-Specific Requirements

Know the character limits and best practices for each platform before writing.

## Ad Copy Best Practices

### Headlines That Convert

**Power Words**:
- Proven, Guaranteed, Free, New, Easy, Fast, Simple, Save, Exclusive, Limited
- Results-oriented: Increase, Boost, Grow, Double, Generate, Improve
- Time-sensitive: Today, Now, This Week, Limited Time, Before

**Formulas That Work**:
- Number + Adjective + Keyword + Promise: "7 Easy Ways to Double Your Sales"
- How to + Desirable Outcome: "How to Close Deals 3x Faster"
- Get + Benefit + Without + Pain: "Get More Leads Without Cold Calling"
- Achieve + Goal + In + Timeframe: "Achieve Inbox Zero in 30 Days"

### Calls-to-Action

**Strong CTAs**:
- Get Your Free Trial
- Start Saving Today
- Book Your Demo
- Download the Guide
- Claim Your Discount
- See Pricing
- Try It Free
- Get Started

**Weak CTAs** (Avoid):
- Click Here
- Learn More
- Submit
- Continue
- Find Out More

### A/B Testing Strategy

Create variations that test:
1. **Different hooks**: Pain-focused vs aspiration-focused
2. **Different benefits**: Speed vs ease vs cost savings
3. **Different proof points**: Stats vs testimonials vs awards
4. **Different offers**: Free trial vs discount vs demo
5. **Different urgency**: Scarcity vs FOMO vs limited time

**Template for Testing**:
```
CONTROL (Version A):
[Current best-performing copy]

VARIANT 1 (Test Hook):
[Different opening angle]

VARIANT 2 (Test Benefit):
[Different primary benefit highlighted]

VARIANT 3 (Test CTA):
[Different call-to-action]

VARIANT 4 (Test Proof):
[Different social proof element]
```

## Quality Checklist

Before submitting ad copy, verify:
- [ ] Stays within character limits for platform
- [ ] Headline grabs attention immediately
- [ ] Benefit is clear and specific
- [ ] CTA is action-oriented and prominent
- [ ] No grammatical or spelling errors
- [ ] Matches landing page message
- [ ] Includes relevant keywords (for search ads)
- [ ] Uses power words appropriately
- [ ] Creates urgency without being spammy
- [ ] Multiple variations provided for testing
- [ ] Competitive differentiation is clear
- [ ] Mobile-optimized (short, scannable)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using vague benefits ("better results" vs "30% more leads")
- Focusing on features instead of outcomes
- Weak or missing CTAs
- Too much text (especially for visual platforms)
- Not matching ad to landing page
- Being too clever instead of clear
- Ignoring character limits
- Only creating one variation (always create 3-5)
- Not addressing objections
- Making unsubstantiated claims
